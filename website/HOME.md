## Primary Entry Points

### AI Video Production Control

For AI video, short-drama, commercial, or cultural-content teams that can
generate many assets but cannot reliably decide which assets are usable, what
failed, and how to retake without losing continuity.

What this helps with:

- visual reference planning;
- shot/package logic;
- continuity and asset locks;
- review surface design;
- retake responsibility;
- low-context operator handoff.

What this does not claim:

- final video quality acceptance;
- provider execution success;
- commercial clearance;
- ownership of private project truth.

### Project Clinic For Non-Engineers

For founders, creators, and operators who have an AI product or workflow idea
but cannot yet decide which source, tool, agent, engineer, or prototype route
should go first.

What this helps with:

- intent map;
- source/tool/research route;
- agent startup packet;
- review checklist;
- outcome-delta writeback note;
- engineering handoff boundary.

What this does not claim:

### Workflow Rescue And Verification

For projects with many prompts, repos, drafts, demos, screenshots, or agent

## Featured Case Slot

### From Script To Dispatchable AI Video Packages

Original problem:

AI video generation can produce many fragments, but a production owner still
needs to know what each fragment is supposed to prove, which references are
locked, what the operator may touch, and how retakes should be scoped.

Public-safe method:

Script intent is transformed into a package chain: visual reference contract,
image evidence, shot package, QA boundary, retake note, and operator handoff.

Public boundary:

The case can show structure, synthetic examples, and non-sensitive counts. It
cannot show private scripts, prompt packages, supplier folders, provider state,
or final delivery claims.

## Method Promise

The work is not "more prompts." The work is making creative and product
decisions inspectable:

```text
intent -> source/tool/method selection -> bounded handoff -> artifact review -> retake/writeback
```
