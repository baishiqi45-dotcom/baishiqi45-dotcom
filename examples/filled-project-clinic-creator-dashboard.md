# Filled Example: Project Clinic Startup Packet

This is an invented public-safe example for a non-engineer creator who wants to
build an AI workflow dashboard but does not know the first safe action.

## Original Intent

```text
What the operator wants:
A dashboard that turns short-drama ideas into AI video task packets.

Why now:
The operator is spending too much time rewriting prompts after each failed clip.

What would be useful if this worked:
One place to decide scene intent, continuity locks, asset roles, and retake scope.
```

## Source Map

| Source | Exists? | Owner | Can be shared with agent? | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Candidate Route

```text
Research first: no, enough method exists for a small packet.
Prototype first: later, after the packet is tested on one synthetic scene.
Design first: no full UI yet; use a markdown packet first.
Engineer first: no, source and acceptance are not clear enough.
Stop or defer: no, but restrict scope to one synthetic scene.
```

## First Safe Action

```text
Agent/task:
Create a one-scene markdown startup packet using the public continuity-lock and
DirectorShotIR templates.

Allowed sources:
This synthetic example, public templates, and owner-approved public wording.

Forbidden sources:
Private scripts, raw video, provider screenshots, account state, and customer
material.

Expected artifact:
One filled packet with scene intent, source map, continuity locks, review rule,
and retake target.

Review checklist:
Can a stranger identify what to generate, what not to change, and what evidence
would be needed before edit review?

Stop condition:
If the packet needs private assets to make sense, do not publish it.
```

## Outcome-Delta Stub

```text
What changed after this attempt:
The idea moved from "build a dashboard" to "test whether one packet can guide
one synthetic scene."

What evidence supports that:
The packet names source, allowed scope, forbidden material, review checklist,
and stop condition.

What should be reused:
The source map and review checklist.

What should be blocked:
Starting UI/backend work before a public-safe packet works.
```

## Boundary

This packet does not prove product-market fit, runtime readiness, engineering
completeness, or final acceptance.
